# Permafrost Degradation in the Siberian Arctic

Sole-researcher independent project on long-term Arctic soil temperature analysis and permafrost degradation forecasting.

## Overview

This project studied long-term soil thaw dynamics in the Siberian Arctic.

As part of the research, I compared 45 years of World Meteorological Organization climate records with data from a local Arctic weather station.

The analysis focused on the relationship between air temperature and soil temperature, long-term warming trends, and the possible approach of critical thawing thresholds.

The project also considered potential risks for northern territories and industrial infrastructure.

## Scope of Work

The project covered the full research cycle:

- fieldwork at an Arctic weather station;
- collection of long-term climate and soil temperature data;
- cleaning and alignment of time-series data;
- statistical analysis of temperature relationships;
- trend visualization;
- forecasting of critical soil temperature thresholds;
- presentation of results to academic and public-sector audiences.
